Detailed nutrition science and real-world case studies.

Essential Nutrient Groups

🍗

Bio-available Protein

Animals need Arginine to process ammonia. A single meal lacking this can cause toxic ammonia buildup in a cat's bloodstream within hours.

🥦

Insoluble Fiber

Vegetables like broccoli and kale contain Sulforaphane, which helps detoxify the liver, but should never exceed 10% of total daily intake.

Feeding Case Studies

🍫
Toxicity Case #01

The Theobromine Crisis

Scenario: A 10kg dog accidentally ingested a dark chocolate bar.

Science: Chocolate contains Theobromine. Humans metabolize it fast, but dogs do it so slowly that it builds up to toxic levels, leading to severe tachycardia (racing heart) and central nervous system failure.

🧅
Biochemistry Case #02

Oxidative Damage via Alliums

Scenario: A cat was fed soup containing hidden onion powder over 3 days.

Science: N-propyl disulfide in onions causes Heinz Body Anemia. It literally makes the animal's red blood cells explode from the inside, leading to oxygen deprivation in vital organs.

🐟
Parasitology Case #03

The Raw Fish Risk

Scenario: Feeding raw salmon to pets in coastal areas.

Science: Raw fish can carry Thiaminase, an enzyme that destroys Vitamin B1. Long-term raw fish diets lead to neurological "Star-Gazing" syndrome where the animal loses control of its neck muscles.
